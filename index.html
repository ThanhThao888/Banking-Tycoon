VOCAB REFERENCE
12 Key Terms
All terms used in the 20 questions.
Retail Bank = Ngân hàng Bán lẻ
Serves individual customers — savings, personal loans, mortgages, credit cards.
Net Interest Margin (NIM) = Biên lãi suất thuần
Loan rate − Deposit rate = Bank profit. E.g. 9% − 4% = 5% NIM.
Fixed Deposit = Tiết kiệm có kỳ hạn
Money locked for a set period (e.g. 12 months) for higher interest.
Savings Account = Tiết kiệm không kỳ hạn
Withdraw anytime. Lower interest rate. High liquidity.
Investment Bank = Ngân hàng Đầu tư
Helps companies raise capital via stocks/bonds. Advises on M&A. Does NOT take public deposits.
Mortgage = Vay mua nhà
Long-term loan to buy property. The property is the collateral.
Collateral = Tài sản thế chấp
Asset pledged to secure a loan. If borrower defaults, bank takes it.
Debit Card = Thẻ ghi nợ
Spends money you already have in your account. No debt.
Credit Card = Thẻ tín dụng
Bank lends you a limit. Spend now, pay back later. High interest if late.
SWIFT = Mạng chuyển tiền quốc tế
Global network for cross-border bank payments.
Trade Finance = Tài trợ thương mại
Banking services that help businesses manage risk in international payments.
Overdraft = Thấu chi
Allows a customer to withdraw more than their balance. Short-term, high interest.
Forward Transaction = Giao dịch kỳ hạn
Lock today's exchange rate for a future transaction. Protects against currency risk.
Spot Transaction = Giao dịch giao ngay
Exchange currency immediately at today's market rate.
Lender of Last Resort = Người cho vay cuối cùng
The Central Bank's emergency role — rescues banks when no one else will lend.
Liquidity Risk = Rủi ro thanh khoản
Bank has assets but no immediate cash to pay customers.
Solvency Risk = Rủi ro mất khả năng thanh toán
Liabilities > Assets. The bank is technically bankrupt.
Bank Run = Rút tiền ồ ạt
Massive simultaneous withdrawals triggered by panic or rumours.
Bailout = Gói cứu trợ
Government injects capital to rescue a failing bank. "Too big to fail."
Devaluation = Phá giá đồng tiền
Government intentionally weakens the currency to boost exports.
